POS Daily Procedures

Granville Tea — Reference Guide for Staff

OPENING PROCEDURES

1. Before Opening

  • Count the cash float and confirm the amount (default $200.00)
  • Place the float in the cash drawer
  • Make sure the receipt printer and card terminal are powered on

2. Open the Shift

  1. 1 Go to pos.granvilletea.com and log in with your username and password (or PIN)
  2. 2 On the Open Shift screen, confirm the terminal name shown (the system remembers which machine you're on)
  3. 3 Enter the Opening Float amount (the cash you placed in the drawer)
  4. 4 Click "Start Shift" — you'll go straight to the sales terminal
Note: Only one shift can be open per terminal at a time. If a shift is already open on your terminal, you'll be taken directly to the staff picker.

3. Switching Between Staff

Multiple staff can use the same terminal during a shift:

  1. 1 Click "Switch User" in the top navigation bar (or wait for the idle timeout)
  2. 2 The Staff Picker appears — tap your name
  3. 3 Enter your PIN if prompted (1–3 digits)
  4. 4 You're now on the terminal — the shift stays open, and your name appears on transactions
Tip: The system automatically returns to the staff picker after a period of inactivity, so you don't need to manually switch if someone walks away.

CLOSING PROCEDURES

4. Before Closing

  • Finish all pending transactions — no open sales in the cart
  • Process any outstanding refunds or voids
  • Make sure all card transactions have been settled (check terminal)

5. End of Day

  1. 1 Click "Admin" in the top bar, enter your manager PIN, then click "End of Day"
  2. 2 Review the Shift Summary displayed on screen:
    • Total number of transactions
    • Total sales amount
    • Payment breakdown (cash, card, gift card)
  3. 3 Count the cash in the drawer — count all bills and coins carefully
  4. 4 Enter the counted amount in the "Closing Cash Count" field
  5. 5 Add any notes if needed (e.g. "broke a coin roll", "paid supplier from drawer")
  6. 6 Click "End of Day" to submit
Tip: Count the cash twice to be sure. Include the opening float in your count — the system accounts for it automatically.

6. Understanding the Shift Report

After closing, the Shift Report appears automatically. Here's what it shows:

Section What It Shows
Shift Times When the shift was opened and closed
Transaction Summary Number of sales, voids, and refunds
Sales Breakdown Subtotal, GST (5%), PST (7%), and total
Payment Breakdown Totals by payment method (cash, card, gift card)
Cash Reconciliation Expected cash vs. your count (see below)
Transaction List Every transaction from the shift

7. Cash Reconciliation

The system automatically calculates what should be in the drawer:

Expected Cash = Opening Float + Cash Sales − Cash Refunds − Petty Cash

Then it compares your count to the expected amount:

Over/Short = Your Cash Count − Expected Cash
Result Meaning Display
$0.00 Drawer is perfectly balanced Green
+$2.50 Drawer is over by $2.50 Green
−$5.00 Drawer is short by $5.00 Red

Example

Opening Float$200.00
+ Cash Sales$485.00
− Cash Refunds$15.00
− Petty Cash$5.00
= Expected Cash$665.00
Your Count$663.50
Over/Short−$1.50
If the drawer is short or over: Double-check your count. If it's still off, note the discrepancy — a manager will review it in Shift History.

8. After Closing

  • Review the shift report for accuracy
  • Remove cash from the drawer (leave the float for the next shift if applicable)
  • Secure the cash according to store policy
  • Log out of the POS
